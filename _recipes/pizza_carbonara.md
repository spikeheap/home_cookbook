name: Carbonara pizza
cuisine: Italian
meal: Main
effort: weeknight
tags: [pizza]
description: A no-tomato pizza with crisped guanciale, two cheeses, and an egg yolk dropped onto the hot crust.

prepTime: PT15M
cookTime: PT10M
recipeYield: 1 × 12" pizza

recipeIngredient:
  Base:
  - quantity: 1
    unit: ball
    item: "[pizza dough](pizza_dough_gozney.html)"
  - quantity: 2
    unit: tbsp
    item: crème fraîche (or a thin layer of olive oil if you prefer)
  Toppings:
  - quantity: 60
    unit: g
    item: guanciale or pancetta, cut into small lardons
  - quantity: 80
    unit: g
    item: fior di latte mozzarella, torn into pieces
  - quantity: 30
    unit: g
    item: Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  Finishing — added after baking:
  - quantity: 1
    item: egg yolk
  - item: Plenty of black pepper
  - item: A drizzle of olive oil
recipeInstructions:
- Stretch the dough into a 12" round on a well-floured peel.
- Spread the crème fraîche thinly across the base.
- Scatter the guanciale and torn mozzarella evenly.
- Bake until the crust is charred and the lardons crisped (60–90 seconds in a pizza oven, 8–10 minutes at 250°C in a home oven).
- Slide the pizza off the peel and immediately crack the egg yolk into the centre.
- Shower with grated Pecorino, a generous turn of black pepper, and a final drizzle of olive oil.
- Slice quickly so the yolk is still glossy when it hits the plate.
---
The egg yolk goes on _after_ baking and gets thinned by residual heat, like proper carbonara. If you'd rather have it set, slip the yolk on with 60 seconds of bake left.

Guanciale is best if you can get it; pancetta works; but smoked bacon will skew the flavour towards a different pizza altogether.
